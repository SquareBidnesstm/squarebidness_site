Innovation Fest 2025: From Louisiana to NYC

Four days in New York City putting Square Bidness in bigger rooms. Conversations on funding, distribution, and collaborations — while never letting go of what made us: discipline, quality, and Louisiana roots.

Sep 24, 2025 · 3–4 min read

Innovation Fest 2025 — bigger rooms, same standards.

We came to New York with one goal: show the work. Not noise — discipline. We talked to founders, operators, and investors who respect product, supply chain, and brand that actually lasts. The same message kept landing: Square Bidness is a real company with real standards.

What we went after

Funding: flexible capital to scale manufacturing (multi-head embroidery, better throughput), tighten fulfillment, and expand the line without sacrificing quality.

Partnerships: retailers who appreciate limited runs, and brand collabs that align with our values — American-made where possible, craftsmanship over shortcuts.

Distribution: keeping direct-to-consumer strong while exploring selective wholesale that doesn’t water down the name.

“If it’s stamped Square Bidness, you can wear it in public and still feel like you handled business, proudly.” That’s the filter we brought to every room.

What we heard back

People responded to the clarity. Supima on the Liberty Tee isn’t a marketing line — it’s proof of our approach. Limited color. Measured drops. No corner-cutting to chase a minute of hype.

The story hits: Louisiana discipline, intentional design, and steady execution.

What’s next

We left with follow-ups on capital, a short list of collab targets, and new doors for distribution. The mission stays the same: build the brand the right way and let the product speak.
