Innovation Fest 2025: From Louisiana to NYC

Four days in New York showing Square Bidness to a bigger room. Funding, partnerships, and why we keep repping Louisiana.

We pulled up to Innovation Fest with the same mindset we build with back home: Principles. Morals. Business Done Right. This wasn’t a sightseeing tour — it was about putting the work in front of people who can move it further without bending our standards.

“If it says Square Bidness, you can wear it anywhere and still feel like you handled business.”

What we came to do

Three lanes: (1) meet buyers who respect limited runs, (2) talk manufacturing and timelines with people who understand quality, (3) line up capital/partners that help us scale the right way — not the noisy way.

Highlights from the floor

  • Buyers who get limited runs: two boutique retailers asked about tight allocations, not bulk.
  • Material questions hit: the Supima story (top ~1% U.S. cotton) landed with product folks.
  • Manufacturing convo: multi-head embroidery = faster turns without losing quality.
  • Distribution fit: selective wholesale that protects brand value over splashy orders.
  • Brand filter: “Would I wear this to handle business?” — that line stuck with people.

Why NYC matters for a Louisiana brand

The room is bigger, the questions are sharper, and the standard sits higher. That matches how we operate. We’re not chasing volume; we’re building reputation. Every piece carries our name — that’s why the fabric, the cut, and the delivery have to stay tight.

Innovation Fest — FAQ

What did we go to NYC to accomplish?

Show the work, build relationships, and align capital/partners who value disciplined growth and quality manufacturing.

How does this help customers right now?

Better throughput on embroidery and fulfillment, more consistent drops, and tighter QC so what ships is ready to be worn anywhere.

Are collabs on the table?

Yes — when the values match. Limited runs, craftsmanship, and brand integrity first.

What’s next?

Follow-ups with funding and retail partners, a refined drop calendar, and expanded Supima/technical pieces.
