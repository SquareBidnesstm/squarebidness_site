You Never Know Who’s Watching

A reflection on leadership, consistency, and the reminder that students notice far more than adults sometimes realize.

By Latrisha Augustine-Pickett Published May 2026

One of our students gave me a card with this inside:

5 things I like about you:

🩶 You are fierce.
🩶 You never let us down.
🩶 You always enforce the dress code.
🩶 You always correct misbehaved students.
🩶 You are funny.

“Keep doing what you do; you never know who’s watching.”
The Principal’s Desk

Moments like that matter more than people realize.

In education, there are long days, difficult conversations, constant decisions, and responsibilities that never really pause. Sometimes you wonder if the consistency is even being noticed.

Then a student reminds you.

They notice the standards. They notice the accountability. They notice who shows up every day the same way. Even when it feels routine to us, it can still make a lasting impact on them.

Leadership in schools is not always about speeches or big moments. Sometimes it is simply about being dependable enough that students know what to expect when they walk through the doors.

That card was a reminder: consistency matters, presence matters, and students are always paying attention.

Keep doing the work. Even on the days when you think nobody sees it.
