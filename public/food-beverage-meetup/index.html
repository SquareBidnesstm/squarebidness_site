Access to Capital: Food CPG. Meet investors, make connections, and hear what it really takes to raise capital in today’s food CPG landscape.

Your opening

Square Bidness helps food founders capture the ordering, payment, customer, and sales proof that makes investor conversations less vague and more grounded.

Targets

Do not chase the whole room. Find the signal.

Food founders

Listen for order chaos, inconsistent payment flow, catering requests, lack of customer records, and no clean way to show demand.

Investors and capital people

Listen for what they call “ready”: repeat purchase, sales velocity, margin clarity, production discipline, and clean data.

Ecosystem leaders

Ask where food founders need support after events: systems, payments, data, follow-up, packaging, and capital readiness.

Your filter

Spend time where there is movement, need, or access. Be warm, but protect your focus.

Wednesday Mission

Show up as the systems layer for food businesses trying to grow.

Position

Square Bidness builds digital ordering, payment, onboarding, and operator-control systems for restaurants, food trucks, lounges, and food businesses.

Capital angle

Investors want evidence. Your systems help food operators capture the data that makes growth, repeat customers, sales velocity, and demand easier to explain.

Operator angle

Many food businesses are still running through texts, calls, DMs, paper notes, and cash. Square Bidness gives them clean lanes for orders, pickup, deposits, menus, and alerts.

Do not lead with

“I make websites.” Lead with systems, payments, data capture, and business operations. The site is the front door. The workflow is the value.

Ask Smart Questions

Questions that put Square Bidness in the right conversation

For investors

  • For food CPG founders, what proof matters most before capital becomes realistic?
  • How much do clean sales and customer data affect your confidence in a food business?
  • Do you see operational systems as part of capital readiness?
  • What makes a food founder look prepared instead of just passionate?
  • Where do early food brands usually break down: production, sales, distribution, or data?

For food operators

  • Where do orders come from today: walk-up, phone, text, DMs, events, or online?
  • What is the hardest part to track: sales, inventory, pickup, staffing, or customer follow-up?
  • If you had cleaner order and payment data, what would you use it for?

For ecosystem leaders

  • What support exists for food businesses trying to become investment-ready?
  • Are there programs helping food brands modernize operations before they raise?
  • Where does technology usually break down for early food founders?
  • How can service providers better support founders after events like this?
