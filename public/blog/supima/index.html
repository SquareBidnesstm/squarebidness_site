Why We Chose Supima Cotton

Anybody can print a logo on a tee. We’re not “anybody.” Supima is the top ~1% of U.S.-grown cotton — softer, stronger, longer-lasting. If our name on it, it can’t feel disposable.

Let’s say this first: Square Bidness is not fast fashion. We don’t cut corners to get something “out.” We don’t hide behind hype graphics and weak blanks. If you’re wearing our work, you’re basically co-signing our name. So the fabric has to live up to that.

That’s why the Liberty Tee is made with real Supima cotton. Supima is an American-grown, extra-long staple cotton fiber. Only about ~1% of cotton grown in the U.S. qualifies. Translation: this isn’t the usual gas station tee.

Liberty Collection — shot in Battery Park, New York City. Built on Louisiana discipline.

So what actually makes Supima different?

1. Softer handfeel, no scratch.
Those longer fibers = smoother surface. You don’t get that rough, dry feel. It lays right on skin, even fresh out the pack.

2. Color that holds.
Cheaper cotton fades fast and starts looking tired. Supima absorbs and retains color better, so our Liberty Green doesn’t wash out after two weekends.

3. Built to last.
Longer fiber = stronger yarn. Stronger yarn = less twisting, less pilling, less sagging neck, less “this used to fit” energy. You keep the shape you paid for.

“If it's stamped with ‘Square Bidness’, that means you can wear it in public and still feel like you handled business.” That’s the standard. That’s why we chose Supima.

Why this matters for us (and you)

We’re building a Louisiana brand with real discipline behind it. We’re not trying to be the loudest shirt in the room — we’re trying to be the one that still looks good next year.

When you buy from us, you’re not just buying cotton. You’re buying proof that we respect our own name enough not to sell you junk.
