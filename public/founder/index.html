Story & Background

Marcus Buckner is the founder of Square Bidness Apparel and SB Tech Lab, a Louisiana-rooted ecosystem that lives where lifestyle, infrastructure, and technology overlap.

What started as limited runs of Square Bidness apparel — worn by everyday people handling real business — has expanded into a multi-site operation with **HQ1 in Hammond**, **HQ2 in Pineville**, and an active **Tech Lab** on the same Hammond property. Along the way, Marcus has invested sweat, time, and capital into owning as much of the process as possible: from wiring outlets and recessed lighting to shaping the brand’s web presence and lender-ready documentation.

He’s a veteran, a builder, and a founder who doesn’t mind getting dusty — because the work he does on ladders and in attics shows up later in contracts, appraisals, reels, and media.

HQ1, HQ2 & Tech Lab

Marcus runs Square Bidness like a real operator:

  • HQ1 — Hammond: Main address, production, living, and decision center.
  • HQ2 — Pineville: Field lab for electrical work, recessed layouts, and LVP corrections.
  • Tech Lab Node: Existing building + 40×50 slab at 46331 N Baptist Rd.

Every upgrade — breakers, outlets, fans, lights — is documented through **SB Tech Lab** so lenders, partners, and media can see real progress, not just captions.

Square Bidness Install Method

The **Square Bidness Install Method** is the discipline Marcus uses to tie everything together — electrical, infrastructure, and how the brand shows up on site.

  • Quality over speed — one clean pass at a time.
  • Documented work — breaker maps, wall-plate counts, recessed layouts.
  • Safety and clarity — no shortcuts on power or structure.
  • Brand in the field — Square Bidness workwear on real jobs.

Media, Recognition & Next Moves

Marcus’s work and story have drawn attention from business and editorial platforms, including recognition through **Marquis Who’s Who** and collaboration with brand strategists focused on long-term positioning, personal brand, and infrastructure ownership.
