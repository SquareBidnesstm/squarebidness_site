Founder & Brand Story

About the Founder — Marcus Buckner

Marcus Buckner is the founder of Square Bidness Apparel and Square Bidness Tech Lab — a veteran-led brand and infrastructure project rooted in Louisiana.

About the Brand — Square Bidness Apparel

Square Bidness is built around the idea that doing good business is a lifestyle. Collections like VSOP and Winter Games pair storytelling with premium materials and limited runs tied to real community motion.

Infrastructure & Footprint

Square Bidness operates a multi-site footprint in Louisiana, supported by company-owned land, facilities, and a phased infrastructure strategy through the Square Bidness Tech Lab.

HQ1 — Hammond (Primary Headquarters)

Operations · Production · Planning

  • Located on approximately 1.07 acres owned by the company
  • Daily operations, production, and content hub
  • Includes a completed 40×50 (approx. 2,000 sq ft) concrete slab reserved for Phase II Tech Lab expansion

HQ2 — Pineville (Field Lab)

Training · Testing · Buildout Documentation

  • Secondary headquarters and active field lab
  • Hands-on electrical, flooring, lighting, and renovation work
  • Used to document real-world execution through the Tech Lab

Square Bidness Tech Lab — Phased Development

The Square Bidness Tech Lab follows a phased development approach designed to prioritize asset ownership, documentation, and disciplined scaling.

Phase I — Foundation & Documentation (Completed)

Phase I established operational control through documented field work, hands-on electrical and construction projects, and internal systems for execution. This phase includes HQ2 as an active field lab and ongoing documentation of real infrastructure work.

Phase II — Expansion & Dedicated Lab Space (Site-Ready)

Phase II centers on expansion into a dedicated Tech Lab facility. A completed 40×50 concrete slab is already in place on the HQ1 property, reserved for modular lab buildout, equipment staging, and future infrastructure systems.

Phase III — Operational Scaling & Asset Utilization (Planned)

Phase III focuses on scaling operations through the use of additional company-owned assets, including a separate one-acre property with an existing building, prepared as a future operational site as demand and capital allow.

This phased approach allows Square Bidness to expand responsibly while maintaining control, transparency, and long-term ownership across its infrastructure.
