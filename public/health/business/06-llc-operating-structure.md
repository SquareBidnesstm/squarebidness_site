# Square Bidness Health, LLC — Operating Structure

## Entity Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Entity type | Louisiana Limited Liability Company |
| Funding model | Self-funded, grant-seeking |
| Business model | CNA float pool — per-shift staffing fees from facilities |
| Revenue source | Facility staffing contracts (per shift or per placement) |
| Clinical model | All placements RN-supervised |

---

## Revenue Model

**Primary revenue:** Facility staffing fees
- Charge facility: market rate per CNA shift (Louisiana average $20–$35/hour for CNA, markup to facility typically $32–$55/hour depending on shift type and urgency)
- Pay CNA: W-2 employee model strongly preferred (see compliance note below)
- RN supervision cost: covered by LLC as cost of operations
- Margin: spread between CNA wage + burden and billing rate

**Secondary revenue (future):** Per-placement fee for permanent hires out of float pool to facilities wanting to convert a temp CNA to staff.

**Note on W-2 vs 1099:**
Classify CNAs as **W-2 employees**, not 1099 independent contractors. Reasons:
1. Louisiana Department of Health and state Board of Nursing expect employee relationships for licensed/certified healthcare workers in supervised settings
2. Grant programs strongly favor employee models (demonstrates workforce development, not just placement brokering)
3. Workers' comp coverage is mandatory for employees — and in healthcare, this is non-negotiable
4. IRS scrutiny on 1099 misclassification is high in healthcare staffing

## Clinical Oversight Structure

```
Square Bidness Health, LLC
        │
        ├── RN #1 — Lead Clinical Supervisor
        │     • Conducts initial facility site visits
        │     • Develops and reviews CNA care plans
        │     • Performs CNA supervisory visits (required under Louisiana BASA standards)
        │     • Signs off on Medicare/Medicaid compliance documentation
        │
        ├── RN #2 — Quality and Compliance
        │     • CNA performance review and documentation
        │     • Incident reporting and follow-up
        │     • Continuing education coordination
        │     • Backup clinical supervisor for RN #1
        │
        └── CNA Float Pool
              • Verified Louisiana CNA certification (check La. Nurse Aide Registry)
              • Background check (Louisiana State Police Level 2 or equivalent)
              • Drug screen
              • References (2 prior healthcare employers minimum)
              • PPD / TB test current
              • CPR/BLS current
```

## CNA Onboarding Requirements (Every New Pool Member)

- [ ] Louisiana CNA registry verification at ldh.la.gov/page/nurse-aide-registry
- [ ] Louisiana State Police background check (or equivalent Level 2)
- [ ] Drug screen (10-panel)
- [ ] 2 healthcare professional references
- [ ] PPD / TB skin test or chest X-ray (within 12 months)
- [ ] CPR / BLS certification (current, American Heart Association preferred)
- [ ] I-9 employment eligibility verification
- [ ] W-4 (W-2 employee) or enrollment in payroll system
- [ ] Workers' compensation enrollment
- [ ] Skills assessment interview with RN supervisor
- [ ] Facility orientation checklist (completed before first shift at any new facility)

## Facility Contract Structure

Each facility relationship should have a written staffing services agreement covering:
- Scope of services (CNA classifications, shift types covered)
- Billing rates by shift type (day/evening/night/weekend/holiday differentials)
- Minimum notice requirements for shift requests
- Cancellation policy
- CNA credential verification requirements
- RN supervisory visit schedule
- Incident reporting protocol
- Non-solicitation clause (prevents facility from hiring your CNAs direct for [X] months)
- Term and termination provisions
- Insurance requirements (both parties)

**Start with a simple 2-page agreement.** A Louisiana-licensed attorney who does healthcare or employment law should review before you execute with your first facility.

## Financial Infrastructure

**Accounts to maintain:**
1. **Operating account** — payroll, day-to-day expenses
2. **Payroll account** — fund separately to ensure payroll obligations are always met
3. **Reserve account** — target 60 days of operating expenses (payroll is the dominant cost)
4. **Grant account** — if/when grant funds are received, maintain separate accounting as required by most grant terms

**Payroll considerations:**
- CNAs on the float pool are paid for hours worked; payroll runs weekly or biweekly
- Consider a payroll service: Gusto, ADP Run, or Paychex — these handle W-2, tax deposits, and workers' comp integration
- Weekly payroll is often preferred by CNA workers (improves retention)

## Insurance Requirements

| Coverage | Minimum | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| General Liability | $1M per occurrence / $3M aggregate | Some facilities require $2M/$4M — check contracts |
| Professional Liability / E&O | $1M per occurrence | Clinical staffing specific |
| Workers' Compensation | Per Louisiana statutory limits | Required for all W-2 employees |
| Employment Practices Liability | $500K minimum | Covers discrimination, harassment, wrongful termination claims |
| Commercial Auto | If staff drive company vehicles | |

Louisiana healthcare staffing insurance brokers: ask for referrals from Louisiana Association of Business and Industry (LABI) or the Louisiana Staffing Association.
