Forward Thinking · AI-Native Operations

How We Inject Technology
Into Every Facility We Enter

We are not a traditional staffing agency that happens to use software. We are an AI-native company — and our staff will know what that means before they ever set foot in a facility. This document captures the vision so it doesn't get lost.

Noted: June 19, 2026 · Marcus Buckner · Square Bidness Health

The Core Idea

Most rural healthcare staffing still runs on phone calls, paper sign-in sheets, and fax machines. We walk in with even basic digital infrastructure and we're already 10 years ahead of what these facilities are used to from any agency they've worked with.

The goal isn't to overwhelm facilities with technology. It's to make every interaction with Square Bidness Health staff feel cleaner, faster, and more professional than anything they've experienced from a staffing company. We set that standard early — so employees know what is expected before they step in the door.

1

Before the Shift

How we prepare staff and facilities before anyone arrives

Text Confirmation — No Phone Tag

CNA gets a text confirmation with shift details, facility address, unit, and charge nurse name. They confirm with a reply. Facility coordinator gets a notification. No calls back and forth, no miscommunication on start time or location.

Build Now

Digital Credential QR Badge

Every Square Bidness CNA carries a badge with a QR code. Charge nurse scans it and instantly sees name, cert number, expiry date, RN supervisor on file, and placement history. No calling the agency to verify. Instant trust.

Next Phase

Pre-Shift Briefing via SMS

30 minutes before shift start, CNA gets a brief automated text: facility name, unit, charge nurse name, what to bring, and a reminder to check in digitally on arrival. Sets the standard from the first interaction.

Next Phase
2

Check-In & Check-Out

Replacing paper logs with real-time digital records

Geofenced Mobile Check-In

CNA opens the Square Bidness Health app and taps check-in when they arrive at the facility. GPS confirms location. Timestamp logged instantly. Facility coordinator sees it in real time — no paper log, no "did they show up?" calls to the agency.

Next Phase

QR Kiosk at High-Volume Facilities

At facilities we staff regularly, we provide a simple tablet kiosk. CNA scans their badge QR on arrival and departure. Automatic time log sent to both Square Bidness Health and the facility coordinator. Clean, verifiable, no disputes on hours.

Down the Road
3

Shift Documentation

The big one — removing the friction from care notes

Voice-to-Text Shift Notes

CNA speaks into their phone at the end of a shift. AI transcribes, structures, and formats it into a proper care note — patient observations, tasks completed, anything notable. Most CNAs dread documentation. We remove that friction entirely. The facility gets better records than they'd get from their own staff.

Next Phase

Structured Digital Handoff

When a Square Bidness CNA's shift ends, a structured digital handoff goes to the charge nurse. Not verbal in the hallway — written, logged, timestamped. The incoming nurse knows exactly what happened. This alone makes our CNAs more valuable than agency staff anywhere else.

Next Phase

Incident Reporting — Digital, Immediate

If something happens on shift — fall, behavior incident, equipment issue — CNA files a digital report from their phone before they leave the building. Timestamped, attached to the placement record, visible to the RN supervisor instantly. No paper forms, no "we'll file it Monday."

Down the Road
4

The Culture Standard

Setting expectations before day one

Digital Onboarding — Before They Ever Enter a Facility

Every CNA is onboarded digitally. They know the app, the check-in flow, what a shift note looks like, how to report an incident, and what the RN supervisor expects — before their first placement. The expectation is set at hiring, not after a problem occurs.

Build Now

AI-Native Identity From Day One

We tell every hire: we are an AI-native company. That means we use technology to work smarter, document better, and communicate faster. If you're not comfortable with that, this isn't the right agency for you. That filter creates a team that compounds over time.

Culture Now

RN Supervisor AI Review

Our RN supervisors use AI tools to review shift notes and flag anything unusual — patterns in documentation, missed observations, potential incidents before they escalate. The supervisor isn't just signing off — she's running an intelligent review layer over every placement.

Down the Road
5

Why This Compounds

The long-term competitive advantage

Facilities Start to Prefer Our Staff

Not just because our CNAs show up — but because they create less administrative burden. Charge nurses stop dreading agency staff because ours make their jobs easier. That's retention on the facility side. They call us first because working with us is just smoother.

Predictive Staffing

Over time, data from placements tells us which facilities need coverage on which days, which CNAs are most reliable in which parishes, and where shortages are likely before facilities even call. We fill the need before they know they have one.

Down the Road

Nobody Else in This Market Is Doing It

Regional staffing agencies in rural Louisiana are not AI-native. They're running on phone trees and paper. We walk in with digital check-in, structured handoffs, and voice-to-text care notes and we're not catching up to anyone — we're setting the standard.
