Why Private Cloud

SaaS platforms work until they don't. Rural Louisiana has learned that lesson the hard way through every major storm season. We built around the assumption that the grid will fail — and planned accordingly.

We Own the Hardware

No per-seat vendor fees that scale against us as we grow. No platform dependency. No third-party terms-of-service that can shut us down. The infrastructure is ours.

We Control the Data

CNA credentials, placement records, and facility contacts stay on our hardware — not on a SaaS vendor's shared cloud. HIPAA-aligned by design, not by policy checkbox.

Built for Louisiana Weather

Three geographically distributed nodes means no single storm knocks out operations. When the I-10 corridor floods, Node 02 stays up. When Southeast takes a hit, Node 03 holds the archive.

The Catalyst

Louisiana Is Building Independent Infrastructure — So Are We

In May 2026, Governor Landry announced the Applied Digital / CLECO partnership — a major private data center and power investment in Central Louisiana. The state is building independent grid capacity. That announcement validated what we were already doing.

Square Bidness Health started building its private node network before that announcement. We're not following a trend. We're operating from the same conviction: rural Louisiana needs infrastructure it owns, not infrastructure it rents.
