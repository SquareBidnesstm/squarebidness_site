Main script

I appreciate the opportunity to continue moving through the VR&E process. Since our last meeting, I understand that I have been found entitled for services, and that the next phase is developing the specifics of my rehabilitation direction and vocational plan. My service-connected PTSD and related conditions continue to create major barriers in traditional employment settings, especially environments with rigid structure, close supervision, high interaction, and limited flexibility. Because of that, I have been building Square Bidness as a self-employment path that better aligns with my limitations and allows me to function in a more controlled and sustainable environment. Square Bidness originally started through apparel and branding, which has always been an important creative and vocational interest for me. Over time, it expanded into building digital infrastructure and operational systems for independent businesses, including restaurants and service operators. Today, the business includes apparel, branding, ordering systems, intake systems, payment infrastructure, and operational support tools designed to help independent operators function more efficiently. I am not starting from zero. I already have active projects, live systems, operational experience, branding infrastructure, and ongoing business activity. What I am hoping to do now through VR&E is continue building this into a structured and sustainable rehabilitation path that accommodates my limitations while allowing me to remain productive and independent. I am looking forward to understanding what documentation, planning steps, and milestones are needed as we move further into the self-employment process and rehabilitation plan.

Business structure

How to frame Square Bidness

Keep apparel, branding, and digital systems connected under one vocational platform.

Business structure summary

Square Bidness is a self-employment platform built around apparel, branding, and digital infrastructure for independent operators. The apparel side represents the original foundation of the business: brand identity, fashion, creative direction, product development, and customer connection. The digital infrastructure side supports independent businesses with ordering systems, payment systems, intake systems, booking flows, websites, and operational tools. Together, these lanes form one business direction: helping real operators present better, operate cleaner, and manage demand more efficiently.

Apparel / Branding lane

Square Bidness Apparel, fashion, product identity, creative direction, brand presentation, limited runs, and customer-facing storytelling.

Digital systems lane

Ordering pages, intake forms, payment flows, booking systems, kitchen/admin views, automation, and operator infrastructure.

Operator support lane

Helping restaurants, service businesses, and independent operators organize demand, reduce chaos, and run cleaner.

Short answers

Fast responses for the next appointment

Use these when the counselor asks direct questions.

What is your vocational goal?

My vocational goal is to build Square Bidness into a sustainable self-employment path that combines apparel, branding, and digital infrastructure for independent operators.

Why self-employment?

Self-employment gives me more control over my environment, workflow, pace, and triggers. It allows me to work in a way that better accommodates my service-connected limitations while still being productive.

So is this apparel or tech?

It is one Square Bidness platform. Apparel and branding are the original foundation, and the digital systems side grew from helping operators organize payments, ordering, intake, and operations. The two lanes support the same business direction.

Are you starting from scratch?

No. I already have active projects, websites, business systems, apparel history, payment workflows, branding work, and real-world execution. What I need now is structure and support to turn it into a sustainable rehabilitation path.

What support are you looking for?

At this stage, I am looking for guidance on the formal self-employment process, required documentation, planning steps, milestones, training needs, and what support may be appropriate after the plan is reviewed.

Why not traditional employment?

Traditional employment environments have been difficult for me to sustain because of my service-connected limitations, especially close supervision, rigid structure, high interaction, stress, and consistency issues. I am trying to build a work environment I can realistically sustain.
