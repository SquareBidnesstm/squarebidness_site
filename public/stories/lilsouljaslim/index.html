Lil Soulja Slim — Full Circle.

What started as a trip to the MAGIC trade show turned into one of those moments that makes you stop and really look at how life moves. From meeting Soulja Slim in 2001 to linking with Lil Soulja Slim in Vegas in 2025, this one came full circle.

How the story started

In February 2025 I was in Las Vegas for the MAGIC trade show.

It was my first time really moving around again after about three years of being locked in building Square Bidness. Sometimes when you’re building something real, you have to sit down for a while before you can move again.

While I was there, I ran into E CEO from Bosstalk 101. I couldn’t think of his name on the spot, so I just yelled, “Bosstalk! Bosstalk!”

We laughed, chopped it up for a second, got a little footage, and that was that.

Lunch changed the story

Later that day I was still making my rounds through MAGIC — sourcing, networking, taking it all in, still figuring out exactly how I was supposed to be moving through the show.

Around lunch time I went to the cafeteria setup they had, and that’s when I ran into E CEO again — and this time he was sitting with Lil Soulja Slim.

Me and Lil Soulja had never formally met before, but we had mutual connections through my guy Tubby of Famous Inc — one of the baddest tattoo artists I’ve ever seen.

So it wasn’t introductions like, “I’m Marcus” or “I’m RedBoy.” It was just:

“What’s up Lil Soulja.”

“What’s up Red.”

And that was that.

We all sat down and ate lunch together.

Real game from E CEO

I’m grateful for E CEO because he gave some serious game about how to move through the MAGIC show.

He was telling me and Lil Soulja the same thing: go to Sourcing, go to Project, go to Runway, connect and network instead of just being there.

Then he asked me if I had any of my merch with me.

I told him: “Yeah… at the room.”

He just looked at me.

I laughed. He laughed.

I asked him what size he wore and told him: “When I see you tomorrow, I got you.”

The next day

The next day I ran into Lil Soulja Slim again.

I asked where E CEO was, and he told me he was getting ready for the BG interview. I said I had a sweatshirt for him.

Soulja looked at me like: “Damn Red… we homeboys, what’s up?”

I told him I had something at the room and that I was literally next door at the Westgate.

He said: “I’m a come get it.”

We kept chopping for a minute, and I told him about meeting his dad — the late great Soulja Slim — back in 2001 during Bayou Classic weekend at the House of Blues.

I headed back to the room and he said he’d be there in ten minutes.

About ten minutes later, my phone rang.

“Red, here I come.”

He pulled up downstairs, we linked, and we flicked it up.

He invited me to something later, but I was chilling and told him I’d hit him if I changed my mind. That was that.

Where it went full circle

Later, when I put the reel together — directing it, producing it, editing it — I noticed something crazy.

In Vegas, I had on Square Bidness shorts the same color as the Coogi sweater I was wearing back in 2001 in the picture with Soulja Slim.

And Lil Soulja Slim had on a black Square Bidness Polar Express hoodie.

In the 2001 picture with his dad, Soulja Slim had on a black hoodie too.

It wasn’t Square Bidness in 2001. But somehow the colors lined up again.

Twenty-four years later.

That’s when it hit me.

Sometimes life comes full circle in ways you don’t even plan.

Two moments, twenty-four years apart

Respect where it’s due

Rest in Peace to the late great Soulja Slim.

Shoutout to Lil Soulja Slim — that’s my guy.

And respect to E CEO from Bosstalk 101 for the game, the conversation, and for helping make the moment what it was.

Some moments don’t need forcing.

They just happen — and if you’re paying attention, you catch what they mean later. This was one of those.
