From “figuring it out” to an install standard

The first time a breaker got flipped or a wall plate came off, it was simple: learn the craft, protect the house, and keep the bill low. No ego — just curiosity and respect for the work.

Fast forward to the end of 2025 and it’s different now:

  • HQ1 runs recessed cans, 20A circuits, and a cleaner production lane.
  • HQ2 is a living classroom for recessed layouts, LVP corrections, and device swaps.
  • The original building behind HQ1 is the first SB Tech Lab node.
  • Brady’s Alexandria house is a Start2Finish portfolio project — documented like a lender case study.
Square Bidness Install Method is the rule now: clean work, safe work, documented work — the same standard in every room, every house, every slab, every rack.

HQ1 · Hammond — Home base gets disciplined

HQ1 isn’t just “where the mail goes.” It’s where Square Bidness lives day to day — inventory, reels, press work, orders, and planning all run through this address.

  • Four new recessed lights in the production room.
  • Targeted 20A outlets for heater loads and press machines.
  • Ceiling fan installed and balanced in the main living/sleeping lane.
  • Wall repairs patched, primed, and painted — damage from the early days cleaned up.
  • Breaker map and future roof work prepped like a real contractor scope.

HQ1 is where the brand shows up online. Now it also holds up on paper — lender-ready and field-ready at the same time.

HQ2 · Pineville — Field lab and training ground

HQ2 started as another house. It turned into a field lab: a place to practice recessed layouts, LVP corrections, and panel discipline without guessing.

  • Whole-house repaint and LVP replacing the old carpet runs.
  • Systematic outlet and switch upgrades — box by box, room by room.
  • Foyer recessed lighting, dimmer work, and breaker logic you can explain on paper.
  • File cabinet and interior doors coated in Dover White for clean lender photos.

Every move at HQ2 got logged inside the Lab: Start2Finish tools, walkthroughs, box maps, and prep sheets .

Tech Lab building & the 40×50 slab

Behind HQ1 is the original Tech Lab building — the first place Square Bidness started organizing tools, wiring, and equipment like a shop.

  • Secure workspace for reels, wiring practice, and equipment.

  • Foundation for SB Tech Lab documentation and the Install Method language.

And behind that, a 40×50 slab is already poured and paid for — the future home of SB Tech Lab Phase II: data, fabrication, racks, and power.
